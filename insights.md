\# Insights: HR Employee Analysis



\## 1. Workforce Overview



The dataset contains employee records across departments, offices, job titles, and employment status. This gives a structured view of workforce composition.



\## 2. Department Headcount



Department-level headcount shows how employees are distributed across the organization. Departments with higher headcount may require more management support and operational planning.



\## 3. Office Distribution



Office-level analysis shows where employees are located. This can support office capacity planning and regional workforce decisions.



\## 4. Manager Hierarchy



The self-join analysis identifies employee-manager relationships. This is useful for understanding reporting structure and leadership span of control.



\## 5. Salary Distribution



Salary analysis shows average salary and total salary cost by department. This helps HR and Finance understand compensation patterns and payroll budget concentration.



\## 6. Salary Banding



Salary band classification groups employees into junior, associate, senior, manager, and director levels. This helps summarize workforce seniority and compensation structure.



\## 7. Hiring Trend



Hiring trend analysis shows how employee count has grown over time. This helps identify periods of workforce expansion.



\## Final Recommendation



The organization should monitor salary cost concentration, manager span of control, and department-level workforce distribution to support better HR planning.
